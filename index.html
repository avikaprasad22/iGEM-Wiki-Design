WHAT IS iGEM?

iGEM, short for The International Genetically Engineered Machine Foundation was first introduced in January 2003 as a course at MIT (Massachusetts Institute of Technology)! The purpose of the course was to provide a way for students to develop a biological device to make cells blink. The journey was named a summer competition is 2004 and ever since has continued to grow having 353 teams participate as of 2019, ranging from over 40 countries!
